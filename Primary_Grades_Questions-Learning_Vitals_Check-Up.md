**Questions For Parents of Preschoolers / Preprimary**

**Learning Skills Check-Up Primary Grades**  
   
 **1\. Thinking and Problem Solving:**

* When your child gets stuck (simple tasks or play), do they try a different way or immediately ask for your help?  
* Does your child give up quickly when a question feels difficult (especially in maths/word problems)?

 

**2\. Attention and Self-Regulation**

* Does your child get distracted easily and find it hard to stay focused on one task (like homework, reading etc.) for 10–15 minutes?  
* Does your child find it hard to wait for their turn or interrupts when others are speaking (at home, in class, or during play)?

 

**3\. Working Memory**

* Does your child forget multi-step instructions and need you to repeat instructions immediately because they forget the steps? (example: “Get your bottle, put the notebook in the bag, and come back”)  
* How often does your child forget where they kept something just a few minutes ago (pencil, eraser, water bottle)?

 

**4\. Planning Skills (Executive Functions)**

* How often does your child get confused while packing their school bag (misses items, packs wrong things)?  
* How often does your child get confused about what to wear while getting ready to go out?  
* Does your child delay starting homework even when they know what to do and leave homework/home tasks unfinished unless you sit and monitor?

 

**5\. Posture and Body Management**

* How often does your child find it hard to sit steady for homework and gets tired quickly during writing?  
* Does your child avoid writing or struggle to keep handwriting neat and consistent?  
* Does your child trip or fall often while playing?

 

**6\. Locomotor and Movement Fluency**

* Does your child avoid active play (sports etc) or feel hesitant in running games (prefers to sit out or watch)?  
* Does your child get tired quickly during continuous movement (after 5–10 minutes of active play)?

 

**7\. Coordination and Bilateral Integration**

* Does your child struggle with tasks that need both hands to work in a sequence (e.g., tying shoelaces, buttoning a shirt, jacket zips \- where one hand holds and the other pulls)?  
* Does your child struggle to catch a small ball with two hands (hands don’t come together in time and the ball slips through)?  
* Does your child find it hard to jump in a proper rhythm (while playing hopscotch, skipping rope, or other games)?

   
   
**8\. Object Control and Visual Tracking**

* Does your child spill water while pouring from a jug into a glass?  
* Does your child lose track of where they are while reading (skips lines or repeats the same line)?  
* Does your child struggle in bat/racket games (batting in cricket, badminton, etc)
